Mission Summary:
Shiyan 12-02 (SY-12 02, NORAD 50322, COSPAR 2021-129B) is a Chinese experimental satellite launched on December 23, 2021, aboard a Long March 7A rocket from Wenchang alongside its twin Shiyan 12-01. It operates in a near-geostationary orbit (approximately 35,730–35,740 km altitude with ~3.6° inclination) and is officially described for space environment monitoring and technology verification, though it is widely assessed as a maneuverable inspector satellite with classified military/intelligence capabilities. It has demonstrated advanced rendezvous and proximity operations (RPO), notably in the 2022 “cat-and-mouse” encounter with U.S. surveillance satellite USA 270 in GEO, where it performed evasive maneuvers and positioned itself for imaging opportunities. More recently, it has conducted multiple close approaches, including a ~70 km flyby of Iran’s Jam e Jam 1 satellite in April 2026. It remains actively maneuvering and operational as of May 2026.
